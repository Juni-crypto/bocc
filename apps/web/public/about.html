The one-liner: Everyone at an event captures the night from their own seat. BOCC pools every guest's point of view into one gallery, so you relive the whole thing from every angle, not just your own.

The problem

You came home with 40 photos. The night actually had four thousand.

Every guest shot the same event from a different seat, and all of it scatters across camera rolls no one ever shares. The full story existed. It just never landed in one place.

How it works

  1. Host drops a QRCreate an event in seconds. Print it, beam it, share the link.
  2. Guests joinNo account. A name and a quick consent tap, and they are crew.
  3. Everyone shoots their POVEvery guest's angle on the same moment, captured at once.
  4. It all poolsOne live gallery, the whole night from every point of view, updating as it runs.
  5. Find your photosA tap on a face, or one selfie, surfaces the shots you are in.

Under the hood

The AI runs on a self-hosted Immich engine behind our own backend, pointed at storage you control. Face detection, semantic search, and OCR happen on infrastructure you can hold, not a black box you rent.

FAQ

Do guests need to install an app?

No. Guests scan a QR and join in the browser with just a name and a consent tap. A native app exists for a richer experience, but it is never required to join, shoot, or browse.

Is this just face recognition?

No. The product is the pooled point-of-view gallery. Face matching is one convenience feature on top, and it only runs after the guest consents.

Where do the photos live?

On a self-hosted Immich engine pointed at storage you control, behind our backend. Your photos stay yours; nothing depends on a third-party photo cloud.

What kinds of events does it suit?

Weddings, parties, birthdays, sports and tournaments, concerts, and trips. Event-type presets set sensible defaults for caps, geofencing, and video.
